How to Use Compress PDF on YourOwnPDF.com

Reducing PDF file size doesn’t require expensive software. Follow this simple guide to shrink your PDF documents using YourOwnPDF.com’s free online PDF compressor. All processing happens in your browser — your files never leave your device.

1

Upload Your PDF Document

Start by clicking the upload area or dragging a PDF file from your computer and dropping it into the upload zone. The tool accepts PDF files up to 85 MB in size. If you chose the wrong file, simply click Clear to start over.

Upload Screen
2

Select Compression Level & Tool

Choose how much to reduce file size and which engine to use. Low (~25%), Medium (~50%), or High (~75%). Pick a compression tool: pdf-lib and compress.js work on individual images, while jsPDF and PDF.js re-render every page for deeper compression.

Compression Settings
3

Compress & Monitor Progress

Click the Compress PDF button to begin. A progress bar shows real-time status as your file is processed. The tool automatically adjusts compression across multiple attempts. Click Cancel to stop at any time.

Progress Bar
4

Download Your Compressed PDF

When compression finishes, a pop-up shows your original size vs. new size and space saved. Click Download to save the compressed file. The compressed PDF keeps all pages and content but takes up much less disk space.
