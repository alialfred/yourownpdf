How to Use PDF to PNG on YourOwnPDF.com

Turning a PDF document into individual PNG images doesn’t require expensive software. Follow this simple guide to extract each page as a separate PNG using YourOwnPDF.com’s free online PDF to PNG converter. All processing happens in your browser — your files never leave your device.

1

Upload Your PDF Document

Start by clicking the upload area or dragging a PDF file from your computer and dropping it into the dotted box. The tool accepts PDF files up to 85 MB in size. Once uploaded, you’ll see a confirmation icon with your file name and size inside the upload box. If you chose the wrong file, simply click the × button on the file icon to remove it and select a different PDF.

  • Drag and drop your PDF onto the upload zone
  • Click the upload area to open file browser
  • Remove and replace files anytime with the × button
2

Choose Scale

Select the scale that matches your needs:

  • 1x — 72 DPI. Smallest file size, good for previews.
  • 1.5x — 108 DPI. Balanced quality for everyday use.
  • 2x — 144 DPI. High quality for printing or archiving.
  • 3x — 216 DPI. Maximum resolution for professional work.

PNG is a lossless format, so image quality is always preserved. Higher scale produces more detailed images but increases file size proportionally.

3

Convert & Monitor Progress

Click the Convert to PNG button to begin. The converter uses the PDF.js engine to accurately render every PDF element — text, images, shapes, and fonts — directly onto a canvas before exporting as PNG.

  • A progress bar shows real-time status as each page is rendered and saved
  • Processing time depends on page count, complexity, and selected scale
  • Click Cancel at any time to stop the conversion
4

Download Your PNG Images as ZIP

When the conversion finishes, a pop-up displays your results: number of pages and total ZIP size. Click Download to save the ZIP archive immediately, or press OK and use the download button that appears below the progress bar.

  • The ZIP file contains one PNG image per PDF page
  • Files are named page-1.png, page-2.png, etc.
  • Ready to use, share, or upload immediately

Why convert PDF to PNG? PNG images support transparency and lossless compression, making them ideal for graphics, logos, diagrams, and screenshots. Converting a PDF to individual PNG pages lets you extract specific pages without sharing the entire document.
